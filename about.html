Statement of Purpose

By the Editorial Board

The swirling winds shriek in a cackling, crazed cacophony. Thick raindrops spiraling down with fury implode on impact as they pierce the Earth below. Each whipping, wet blow makes our venturing onward more strenuous, yet, through struggle, we hoist our soaking bodies into a moment of calm: the eye of the storm, the center of the spiraling gyre. During this brief moment of respite, staring at the towering walls of white-and-gray that encase us, an assessment of the situation is in order. How did we arrive in the midst of such onslaught? How do we chart on a path out?

This temporary refuge from the chaos of the circling storm is what The Widening Gyre exists to be. We seek to be what Walt Whitman writes as “both in and out of the game and watching and wondering at it.” A necessary relationship exists between the two questions raised by our traveler’s predicament. One cannot simply assess the storm, like some heavenly figure peering down from a cloud with a telescope to extract some eternal truth from it without movement towards freedom. Conversely, to thrust oneself headstrong back into the winds without taking account for present conditions will only lead to devourment by the storm. Reaching the rolling hills of a greener pasture is only possible, in our view, through the interrogation of both questions together — and it is the goal of this magazine to do just that.

Writings of this kind in the era of late-capitalism seem to be almost a necessarily self-indulgent endeavor. The medium of writing, believed by this magazine to be a sacred one, has been eclipsed as a tool for mass dissemination by that of the video and, lesser so, of the audial. However, this magazine exists, primarily, for the writers of these pieces. This refuge exists as a place for writers to work through the aforementioned questions with ink and paper before taking their conclusions into the world around them in their respective lives.

This magazine does not maintain standards of conventional “greatness” in the form or content of its works. The mere practice of creation — human creation, that is — is an assertion of agency against the late-capitalist Machine. Through the pen, writers solidify their thoughts to provide a roadmap to action for themselves, and if readers can glean satisfaction from their writing or draw an applicable conclusion, then all the better. Do not expect perfection, dear reader, for the imperfection found in these pages is proof that the difficult process of sifting through the wreckage of our modern world is occurring.

To truly experience The Widening Gyre one must hold a copy in their hands. Print media plugs the slop-spewing spout of algorithmic and AI-driven recommendations which are optimized for sensation, attention, advertisement, and profit. We do not want to encourage our readers to open a computer more than they otherwise have to, and thus take pleasure in producing physical copies for circulation. For logistical purposes, we may use the Internet, but we only do so with profound reluctance.

The Widening Gyre is not and will never be a profit-seeking endeavor. The magazine will be owned collectively by its editorial board. We are beholden to no bosses, shareholders, politicians, or advertisers, and if we fall victim to greed or decadence, it is the duty of our writers to dismantle this magazine and build it anew.

We reject the rigidity of dogma and ideology worship — we hold no party line. These pages will be a laboratory for writers to work out ideas in service of the advancement of human liberation and freedom. Ideological diversity will arise between the front and back cover, and we hope that muddling through that tension will reveal a more informed path forward.

The Widening Gyre is a human endeavor and will never use the tools of alienation in its creation; to do so is to contaminate the sanctity of our art. We assert emphatically that human creation, no matter how base, is always preferable to the drivel spouted by the Machine. Art is the inward examination of the soul turned outward, and thus unattainable for that which consists of cold gears and predictive algorithms. We deny permission for any aspect of this magazine to be used as fodder for any predictive algorithm or generative AI.

“It will hold as long as we rise to the task of holding it.”
